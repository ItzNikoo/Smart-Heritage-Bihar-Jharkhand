Buxar Fort

Buxar Fort, located on the banks of the Ganga River, is a historic stronghold built in the 11th century. Known for its strategic position and scenic views, it has witnessed several battles, including the famous Battle of Buxar in 1764. The fort’s remains include ancient carvings, temples, and stone walls. It stands today as a reminder of Bihar’s military and cultural past, attracting history lovers and curious travelers from around the region.

History & Significance

The fort is believed to have been built by King Rudra Deo in 1054 CE. Its location along the Ganga made it a crucial defense point and a center of cultural exchange. Over centuries, it became associated with saints like Balmiki and Vishwamitra, adding spiritual value. The 1764 Battle of Buxar, fought nearby, changed the course of Indian history by establishing British control. Today, the fort’s ruins reflect Bihar’s layered heritage and architectural legacy.

Transport

Buxar Fort is easily accessible from Buxar town, about 2 km from the railway station. Buxar lies on the Patna–Varanasi route, with regular train and bus services. The nearest airport is Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport, Varanasi, around 120 km away. Visitors often combine a trip to the fort with nearby ghats, temples, and cultural sites along the Ganga. Local transport like rickshaws and taxis make reaching the fort convenient for tourists and pilgrims alike.
