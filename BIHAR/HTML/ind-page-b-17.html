Maner Sharif

Maner Sharif, located about 30 km west of Patna, is a revered Sufi pilgrimage site famous for the shrines of Hazrat Makhdoom Yahya Maneri and his disciple. Built during the Mughal era, the complex showcases Indo-Islamic architecture with domes, arches, and intricate carvings. The dargah attracts devotees from across India, who come to pay respects and seek blessings. The site also symbolizes Bihar’s spiritual diversity and harmonious cultural traditions.

History & Significance

Maner Sharif is an important Sufi center, with two key shrines known as Bari Dargah and Chhoti Dargah. The larger shrine, built in the 17th century, is the resting place of Hazrat Makhdoom Yahya Maneri. It reflects Mughal architectural brilliance with fine stonework and calligraphy. The site remains a symbol of spiritual devotion, communal harmony, and the cultural fabric of Bihar. Annual Urs festivals draw large numbers of devotees and visitors alike.

Transport

Maner Sharif lies on National Highway 30, making it easily accessible from Patna, just an hour’s drive away. The nearest railway station is Bihta, around 15 km from the site, with connections to major cities. Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport in Patna serves as the nearest airport. Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and buses provide smooth local connectivity. The site can be visited year-round, though winters are the most comfortable season for pilgrimage and sightseeing.
