Rohtas Fort (Rohtasgarh)

Rohtas Fort, also called Rohtasgarh, is a majestic hill fort located in Rohtas district, Bihar. Built in the 7th century by King Harishchandra and later expanded by Sher Shah Suri, it covers nearly 4,000 acres. The fort houses massive gates, palaces, temples, and reservoirs, showcasing remarkable defense architecture. Perched on Kaimur hills, it is both a historical landmark and a scenic site, reflecting the military and cultural heritage of medieval Bihar.

History & Significance

Rohtas Fort’s origins trace back to the 7th century, though it gained prominence under Sher Shah Suri in the 16th century. It was designed as an invincible stronghold with 83 gates, intricate water systems, and military facilities. The fort also includes temples, mosques, and palaces, reflecting the syncretic culture of its time. Today, it remains a UNESCO tentative site and a testament to medieval engineering, standing as one of India’s grandest hill forts.

Transport

Rohtas Fort is located about 39 km from Sasaram in Rohtas district. Visitors can reach Sasaram by train on the Patna–Mughalsarai route, with Sasaram railway station as the nearest stop. The closest airport is Varanasi International Airport, approximately 120 km away. From Sasaram, taxis and jeeps are available to the fort. The uphill journey is scenic but requires a trek, adding adventure for travelers who wish to explore this historic fortification.
