Kesaria Stupa

Kesaria Stupa, located in East Champaran, is one of the tallest and largest Buddhist stupas in the world, rising to about 104 feet. Believed to date back to the Mauryan period, it was expanded during the Gupta era. Associated with Lord Buddha’s last sermon before his Mahaparinirvana, the site is a major pilgrimage for Buddhists. Its historical grandeur, architectural scale, and spiritual aura make it a jewel of Bihar’s Buddhist heritage.

History & Significance

The stupa is believed to have been originally constructed under Emperor Ashoka and later developed during the Gupta period. Excavations by the Archaeological Survey of India revealed terracotta artifacts and Buddhist relics around the site, confirming its religious importance. According to Buddhist tradition, Lord Buddha gave away his begging bowl here before heading to Kushinagar. Today, Kesaria stands as a monumental example of Buddhist architecture and Bihar’s historical legacy.

Transport

Kesaria is located about 110 km from Patna and 40 km from Motihari, well connected by roadways. The nearest railway station is Chakia, about 15 km away, with links to Muzaffarpur and Patna. The closest airport is Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport in Patna. Local buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws make the site approachable for visitors. The best time to visit is during winter months, when the weather is cool and pleasant.
