Kanwar Lake Bird Sanctuary

Kanwar Lake Bird Sanctuary, located in Begusarai, is the largest freshwater oxbow lake in Asia and an important wetland for migratory birds. Spread over nearly 67 square kilometers, it attracts species like bar-headed geese, sarus cranes, and pintail ducks. The sanctuary plays a vital role in biodiversity conservation and is also significant for local communities. Its tranquil waters and rich avian diversity make it a paradise for bird lovers and researchers alike.

Ecological Significance

Kanwar Lake is home to more than 160 bird species, including migratory flocks arriving from Central Asia during winter. The wetland also supports fish species and aquatic plants, contributing to local livelihoods. Recognized as a Ramsar site, the sanctuary highlights the importance of preserving wetland ecosystems. Despite challenges like encroachment and pollution, Kanwar Lake remains a critical habitat for birds, attracting conservationists, eco-tourists, and nature enthusiasts from across India and abroad.

Transport

The sanctuary is situated about 22 km from Begusarai town and approximately 125 km from Patna. The nearest railway station is Begusarai Junction, offering good connectivity to Bihar’s major cities. Road transport is accessible via NH-31, linking Patna to Begusarai. The closest airport is Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport in Patna. Local auto-rickshaws and taxis make the sanctuary approachable, though visitors often prefer guided tours to explore the wetland’s unique biodiversity.
