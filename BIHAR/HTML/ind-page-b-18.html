Sita Kund

Sita Kund, located in Sitamarhi, is a sacred Hindu site believed to mark the birthplace of Goddess Sita from the epic Ramayana. The holy pond is associated with legends of her appearance during King Janaka’s ploughing ritual. Pilgrims visit to take ritual baths in the kund, especially during Ram Navami and other festivals. It remains a deeply revered spot reflecting Bihar’s connection to India’s ancient mythology and devotion.

History & Significance

Sita Kund holds great religious importance as the legendary birthplace of Goddess Sita. According to mythology, she emerged from the earth when King Janaka ploughed the land here. The kund is considered sacred, and bathing in it is believed to cleanse sins. The temple complex nearby hosts large gatherings during Ram Navami and other Hindu festivals. Devotees from all over India flock here, making Sitamarhi a vibrant spiritual hub in Bihar.

Transport

Sitamarhi is well connected by road and rail. Sita Kund is located just a few kilometers from Sitamarhi town center, accessible by auto-rickshaw or taxi. Sitamarhi Junction Railway Station offers direct trains to Patna, Muzaffarpur, and other major cities. The nearest airport is Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport in Patna, about 140 km away. Regular buses and private vehicles make travel convenient for pilgrims visiting the sacred kund throughout the year.
