Dashrath Manjhi Road

Dashrath Manjhi Road, cut through the rocky Gehlour Hills near Gaya, is a symbol of human perseverance. Built single-handedly with hammer and chisel over 22 years, the 110-meter-long passage reduced the distance between Atri and Wazirganj blocks from 55 km to just 15 km. Known as the "Mountain Man Road," it stands as a testament to determination and love, drawing travelers, filmmakers, and tourists who come to witness the path carved by one man for his village’s better future.

History & Significance

Dashrath Manjhi began carving the road in 1960 after losing his wife, who could not access timely medical help due to the hill barrier. Working alone for over two decades, he connected Gehlour to Wazirganj, drastically cutting travel distance. Today, the road is visited as a memorial of courage and commitment, often called the "Mountain Man’s Road." It has been featured in books, films, and documentaries, making it both a historical landmark and an inspiring destination for visitors across the world.

Transport

The road lies in Gehlour village, Gaya district. The nearest railway station is Gaya Junction, about 12 km away. Gaya International Airport, around 20 km, provides domestic and limited international flights. Local buses and autos connect nearby towns, while taxis offer direct access from Gaya city. From Patna, the distance is about 115 km, usually a three-hour drive by road. Visitors can combine the trip with nearby heritage attractions, making the journey both practical and meaningful.
