Rajgir Hot Springs

Rajgir Hot Springs, located at the foothills of Vaibhava Hill, are famous for their warm, mineral-rich waters believed to have therapeutic qualities. The springs are closely linked to Buddhist and Jain traditions, with legends stating that Lord Buddha and Lord Mahavira both visited the site. Surrounded by temples and scenic hills, the springs attract devotees and tourists seeking both spiritual solace and natural healing in the heart of Bihar’s historic Rajgir.

History & Significance

The hot springs of Rajgir are considered sacred, with water emerging from seven different sources, each associated with unique healing properties. The Brahmakund is the most prominent spring, with water temperatures reaching around 45°C. Devotees take ritual baths here, believing the water purifies both body and soul. With connections to ancient Buddhist and Jain traditions, Rajgir’s springs remain a center of faith, health, and cultural heritage, attracting pilgrims and wellness seekers alike.

Transport

Rajgir is well connected by road and rail. The hot springs are located close to the town center, easily accessible by rickshaw, taxi, or on foot. Rajgir Railway Station links the area with Patna, Gaya, and Nalanda. The nearest airport is Gaya International Airport, about 75 km away, while Patna Airport lies 100 km distant. Regular buses and shared vehicles make Rajgir a convenient destination for both pilgrims and tourists year-round.
