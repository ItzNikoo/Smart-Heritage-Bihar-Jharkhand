Kumhrar (Patna)

Kumhrar, located in Patna, is the site of ancient Pataliputra, capital of the Mauryan Empire. Archaeological excavations here revealed the remains of the famous 80-pillared hall built during Ashoka’s reign. The ruins give insight into the grandeur of Mauryan architecture and administration. Today, it stands as a protected site that connects visitors with Bihar’s historical significance, showcasing the ancient city that once stood as a political and cultural powerhouse of India.

History & Significance

Kumhrar’s excavations have unearthed layers of history dating back to 600 BCE. The most remarkable discovery is the 80-pillared hall, believed to be a Mauryan assembly or audience hall built by Emperor Ashoka. The site also reveals remnants of Buddhist monasteries, Gupta-era artifacts, and terracotta figures. Kumhrar highlights the prominence of Pataliputra as a political and cultural hub. Its preserved ruins serve as a gateway into India’s glorious past and the architectural brilliance of early empires.

Transport

Kumhrar is located within Patna city, around 5 km from Patna Junction Railway Station. The site is easily accessible by auto-rickshaws, taxis, and city buses. The nearest airport is Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport, about 10 km away. Visitors can combine a trip to Kumhrar with nearby attractions such as Patna Museum, Golghar, and Gandhi Maidan. With good connectivity and local guides, reaching and exploring Kumhrar is convenient for history lovers and curious travelers.
