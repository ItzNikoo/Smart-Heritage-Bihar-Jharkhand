Wildlife & Ecology

The park covers an area of about 900 square kilometers, forming part of the Valmiki Tiger Reserve. It is home to Bengal tigers, Indian leopards, wild boars, and antelopes, along with reptiles like pythons and gharials in the Gandak River. Over 250 bird species, including peafowls, flycatchers, and storks, enhance its biodiversity. The forest vegetation ranges from sal trees to wetlands, providing a unique ecological balance across the Terai landscape.

Transport

Valmiki National Park is located about 275 km from Patna. The nearest railway station is Valmikinagar, which connects with Gorakhpur and other towns. Road access is available from Bettiah and Motihari via state highways. The closest airport is Gorakhpur Airport, about 100 km away, offering domestic connectivity. Jeep safaris and guided tours are organized for visitors, with eco-tourism facilities available near the park entrance for comfortable stays and exploration.
