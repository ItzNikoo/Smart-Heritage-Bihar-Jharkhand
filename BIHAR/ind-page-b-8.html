Pawapuri (Jal Mandir)

Pawapuri, in Nalanda district, is one of Jainism’s holiest sites where Lord Mahavira attained Nirvana in 527 BCE. The famous Jal Mandir stands in the middle of a lotus-filled pond, connected by a marble pathway. Its tranquil surroundings and sacred atmosphere draw pilgrims and tourists year-round. The temple’s serene waters and symbolic architecture reflect peace, purity, and Bihar’s deep spiritual legacy, making it a treasured destination for faith and history.

History & Significance

Pawapuri gained sacred importance after Lord Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara, attained Nirvana here in 527 BCE. To honor his memory, devotees constructed the Jal Mandir in the middle of a pond where large amounts of soil had been removed by followers, leaving a water body. The temple, built in white marble, symbolizes purity and devotion. Thousands visit every year, especially during Diwali, to celebrate Mahavira’s Nirvana. Its calm environment and spiritual aura make Pawapuri a timeless place of worship and reflection for Jain pilgrims and history lovers.

Transport

Pawapuri is well connected by road, located about 38 km from Rajgir, 20 km from Bihar Sharif, and around 101 km from Patna. Regular buses, taxis, and private vehicles operate from Nalanda, Patna, and Rajgir. The nearest railway station is Bihar Sharif, while Patna’s Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport is the closest air link. Pilgrims can also find dharamshalas and lodges near the temple. Travel to Pawapuri offers a peaceful journey through Bihar’s rural charm, enhancing the spiritual experience of visiting one of Jainism’s most sacred shrines.
