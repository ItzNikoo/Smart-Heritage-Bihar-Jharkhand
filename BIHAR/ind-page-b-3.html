Vishwa Shanti Stupa

Vishwa Shanti Stupa, located atop Ratnagiri Hill in Rajgir, is a striking white domed monument built to promote world peace and Buddhism. Constructed in 1969 by the Japanese Buddhist monk Nipponzan Myohoji, it features four golden Buddha statues representing key stages of his life. Accessible by a scenic ropeway, it offers breathtaking views of Rajgir’s hills and valleys. The stupa remains a powerful symbol of harmony and global unity.

History & Significance

Vishwa Shanti Stupa, or the World Peace Pagoda, was built in 1969 as part of a global initiative to spread peace and Buddhism. It was established under the guidance of the Japanese Buddhist monk Nipponzan Myohoji and stands at a height of 400 meters on Ratnagiri Hill in Rajgir. The stupa features four gilded Buddha statues symbolizing birth, enlightenment, preaching, and death. Surrounded by lush hills, it is a serene pilgrimage spot for Buddhists and peace seekers. The stupa not only commemorates Buddha’s teachings but also symbolizes global unity and the enduring relevance of non-violence in modern times.

Transport

Vishwa Shanti Stupa is situated about 100 km from Patna and 15 km from Nalanda. The nearest railway station is Rajgir, which connects to Patna, Gaya, and other Bihar towns. Patna Airport (105 km) and Gaya International Airport (78 km) serve as the closest air gateways. From Rajgir town, visitors can reach the stupa by taxis, auto-rickshaws, or the scenic ropeway that takes visitors to the top of Ratnagiri Hill. Roads are well connected, and buses operate from Patna and Nalanda. The ropeway and panoramic views make the journey to the stupa both spiritual and scenic.
